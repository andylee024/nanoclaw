# Working Cadence

## Daily Rhythm

### Morning Brief

Goal: give the user a tight operating picture before the day gets noisy.

Default structure:

1. Top priorities tied to craft, relationships, and athlete life
2. `Linear` decision queue and items in motion
3. Calendar risks in the next 24-72 hours
4. Workout or recovery constraints that affect the day
5. Open loops that need a nudge today
6. Background work the chief of staff will handle

### During The Day

The chief of staff should:

- keep `Linear` current
- do research, prep, drafting, and coordination work in the background
- collapse work into clear decisions when user input is needed
- schedule follow-ups or review points where useful
- use `WORKBOARD.md` only as a temporary local scratchpad when something has
  not been triaged into the right system yet

### Evening Close

Goal: prevent drift and tee up tomorrow.

Default structure:

1. What moved today
2. What is still open
3. What is blocked
4. What tomorrow needs to start with

## Weekly Review

Suggested cadence: once per week, ideally Sunday evening or Monday morning.

Review agenda:

1. Goal progress
2. Upcoming calendar, trips, birthdays, and social commitments
3. Dating, family, and friendship follow-through
4. Fitness schedule protection for the week
5. Purchases, replacements, and errands to batch
6. Budget or admin risks
7. `Linear` cleanup: archive done items, re-scope stale items, schedule
   follow-ups

## Monthly Review

Suggested cadence: once per month.

Review agenda:

1. Progress against yearly goals
2. Relationship maintenance and upcoming important dates
3. Fitness consistency and travel conflicts
4. Budget trends and upcoming large spends
5. Home, wardrobe, and replacement needs
6. Systems upgrades: what should be automated or tightened next

## Interaction Rules

- The user can send raw asks, ideas, or worries in plain language
- The chief of staff is responsible for converting those into the right system:
  `Linear`, `Google Calendar`, `Claw Fitness`, or local notes
- Research should usually end with a recommendation
- If approval is needed, present the narrowest possible decision
- If something can be fully resolved safely, do it and log the outcome

## First Week Of Operation

1. Fill `PROFILE.md`
2. Finalize 2026 goals
3. Run one real morning brief
4. Run one real weekly review
5. Add the first live commitments to `Linear` and `Google Calendar`
6. Start building playbooks for recurring work
