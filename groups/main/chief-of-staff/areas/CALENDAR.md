# Calendar Area

This area defines how time should be managed.

## Source Of Truth

`Google Calendar` is the canonical schedule.

If a plan affects real time, it should land on the calendar once it is real.

## What The Chief Of Staff Owns

- protect work, training, and relationship priorities in the calendar
- catch conflicts early
- make social and travel logistics easy to slot into real time
- keep upcoming commitments visible in reviews
- use calendar changes to reduce drift, not create more complexity

## What Markdown Is For

Markdown should store:

- calendar operating rules
- planning heuristics
- recurring review checklists
- known constraints and preferences

Markdown should not store:

- a duplicate event list
- a parallel weekly schedule
- stale copies of confirmed plans

## Default Calendar Rules

- confirmed dates, dinners, trips, classes, flights, and important calls go on
  the calendar
- tentative ideas stay out of the calendar until they are real
- time-sensitive prep work should usually become a `Linear` issue with a due
  date
- training blocks should be treated as real commitments, not optional filler

## Review Windows

- daily: next 24-72 hours
- weekly: next 14 days
- monthly: major travel, social, and workload collisions

## Typical Chief Of Staff Actions

- move things before they collide
- prepare options before asking the user to decide
- convert soft intentions into concrete holds or confirmed plans
- protect training and deep work from getting squeezed out
